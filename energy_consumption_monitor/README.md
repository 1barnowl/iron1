An advanced energy consumption monitor collects real-time and historical energy data from smart meters, sensors, and utility APIs, then stores it in a scalable time-series database for analysis. It uses data pipelines and feature engineering to prepare the data for machine learning models that forecast demand, detect anomalies, and identify inefficiencies. Forecasting models like LSTM and Prophet predict future consumption, while anomaly detection and clustering reveal abnormal behavior, equipment faults, or optimization opportunities. Optimization frameworks such as PyPSA help simulate and schedule energy usage to reduce costs, avoid peak penalties, and improve operational efficiency. Dashboards built with tools like Grafana or Streamlit provide live visualization, alerts, and actionable insights for operators and decision-makers. The system also tracks compute energy usage, supports backtesting with historical data, and uses modular, scalable architecture so it can grow from a single facility to large industrial or multi-site deployments.
