# Iron Core

## Core Idea

Iron1 functions as a distributed, safety‑critical orchestration fabric that unifies the automation of physical, financial, and industrial processes into a single, verifiable execution framework. It ingests canonical telemetry from perception layers and market feeds to drive a suite of deterministic, simulation‑first subsystems—spanning supply chain planning, algorithmic trading, industrial IoT harmonization, inventory balancing, predictive maintenance, and energy consumption optimization—each designed to separate the formulation of intent from the exercise of authority. Every proposed action, whether a warehouse transfer, a trading order, a firmware update, or an actuator command, is first validated against a digital twin or replayable simulation, then subjected to strict promotion gates (simulated → paper → canary → production) that enforce KPI adherence and safety constraints. The core delegates all sovereign decisions—funds movement, contract signing, exposure limits, and compliance vetoes—to the platform’s support cores (Finance, Custody, Risk), ensuring that Iron remains an authoritative proposer and executor rather than an autonomous controller. Through immutable audit trails, signed manifests, and cryptographically attested execution records, Iron Core establishes a non‑repudiable operational ledger that enables forensic replay, continuous verification, and the safe, scalable automation of high‑consequence workflows across the boundaries of the digital and physical economies.
